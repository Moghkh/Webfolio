Conversation with the masters

A Human-Machine dialogical exploration.

‘Conversation with the Masters’ is a Media-installation that conveys the literature and philosophy of the former Bauhaus masters, while exploring the dialogical possibilities between humans and machines.

Overview

Bauhaus oases – trans-local network

This project was an introduction to the MediaArchitecure program in the winter semester 2018/19. It responded to the expectations of visitors to the Bauhaus anniversary year 2019.

Media installations were designed to present information related to authentic Bauhaus-places and domains of the former Bauhaus protagonists in Weimar to make the history present.

These places form a network of trails and could be experienced during a Bauhaus walk. The interactive installations enabled the communication of information through the media sound, light, projection, images, text or video to design a trans-local reality and spatial situations in urban context.

research

literature review and inspirations

In the first part of this project, we analysed the history and story of the Bauhaus places and documented them with drawings, photos, and concept models.

Parallel to the analysis we learned in several seminars and technical workshops how to apply different media tools and technologies.

Ideation

Designing the interaction

In a series of workshops, all students came up with individual ideas, presented them in agile storyboards, and voted on their favorite ones.

We presented these Ideas in a first plenum before picking the best three to be realized.

My concept, ”Conversation with the masters” was one of the three ideas picked to be realized. It featured a network of interactive devices, which can print out messages from the Bauhaus past on small pieces of paper. These messages were carefully selected quotes of the former Bauhaus masters that are still relevant today.

Each device was an abstraction of a persona of a Bauhaus master, which reveals itself through an onscreen interactive dialog. We developed two personas, ‘Walter’ inspired by Walter Gropius the founder of the Bauhaus and ‘Oskar’ inspired by Oskar Schlimmer, the famous Bauhaus artist.

prototyping

Designing the physical interface

We Designed two interactive devices called ‘Walter’ and ‘Oskar’. The souls of these devices are selections of quotes from Walter Gropius and Oskar Schlemmer, which are printed out on small pieces of paper using a tiny thermal printer in each device.

Walter and Oskar display their emotions through moving pieces of fabric suspended in space, mimicking the shape of wormholes, and folded sheets of thin transparent acrylic that move and reflect LED lights.

The color of these LED lights changes according to the ever changing moods of Walter and Oskar.

Both devices run with a microcontroller ‘Arduino Mega’, which controls five servo-motors, moving the fabric and the acrylic sheets during the interactions. The microcontroller also controls two individually addressable LED strips, an LCD display and a thermal printer. It receives sensory data from an array of infrared sensors, two push buttons and an ultra-sonic sensor. The whole circuit is powered using a commercial power bank with two USB outputs; one designated for the thermal printer, while the other powers everything else. The program was written in ‘C++’ using Arduino IDE.

deployment

GO4Spring Exhibition

Both Walter and Oskar were placed side by side in the mediaArchitecture room for the opening of the GO4Spring Exhibition in Weimar 2019.
Visitors of the exhibition interacted with the devices and exchanged printed messages between them.

Gropius Zimmer Pavilion

The installation was exhibited a few days later in coordination with “Gropius Zimmer Pavilion” during the "Woche der Demokratie" event in Weimar. This time Walter was placed in the pavilion and Oskar was placed about 20 meters away in front of the DNT Theater. The interface and dialog was adjusted to the new location, and the printed messages pointed at the new locations with the help of a small map.

evaluation

The GO4Spring exhibition served as a contextual inquiry which revealed the parts of the interaction that were user-friendly and the ones that weren’t.

Our team observed the visitors interacting with the installations and talked to them. Few adjustments were made at the end of every day of the exhibition.

For example: arrows and explanatory lines were added to the LCD conversation, to show the users how to use the buttons or the barcode reader slot to interact with the installation. Some printed messages were changed or eliminated as well when we found them to add confusion to the overall user experience.

The second exhibition at the Pavilion was even better perceived partially due to the fact that both devices were placed at a distance making the user experience more rewarding and enjoyable.

Kids were running to deliver the printed messages from one location to the other, an old lady giggled when the device told her she was nice, and an old man asked about the project but he was a bitconfused when we mentioned Walter Gropius.
