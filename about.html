Heyo, I'm Mo - UX product designer, developer, and a problem solver, striving to create outstanding user experiences in both digital and physical products.

Born and raised on the west coast of Syria. By the age of 18 I moved abroad to Northern Cyprus pursuing a Bachelor degree in Architecture. I started my career thereafter in one of the top architecture studios in Northern Cyprus.

During my work as an architect, I discovered a new passion for technology. This passion drove me to seek a master's degree in Mediaarchitecture at the Bauhaus University in Germany, which I successfully finished in March 2020.

In my master's program, I focused on user-interface (UI), user-centered design and user-research methods. Starting with a semester project with the User Interface (UI) department and an accompanying seminar on mobile development. Additionally, I developed a strong association with the Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) department with a semester project focusing on user research methods leading to my graduation project which was supervised by the head of the (HCI) department.

Architects are UX designers of the physical space:

A career shift from architecture to UX design might sound like a crazy idea in the beginning. But when you really think about it, both professions do have quite a bit in common. As an architect you learn how to balance aesthetics and functionality in a complex design projects, while keeping an eye on the feasibility and economical aspects.

The tools you use as an architect for illustrating your ideas: sketching, 3D modeling, rendering and producing detailed documentations, are very similar to the tools you would need as a UX designer. And of course the most important tool of all is your imagination, and your ability to predict all the different use cases of your product and the problems that might occur and solve them.
